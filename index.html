Why General Assembly?

I first took a programming class in high school; I both enjoyed it and excelled at it. It was something I wanted to continue with, so when I went to college I was initially a computer science major. I found that experience disappointing, because the classes were structured as large lectures with little actual programming, and I ended up switching majors. However, I still find programming problems challenging and enjoyable, and would like a job where that is a major part of my work. I selected General Assembly because I learned from my college experience that I learn best in hands-on team environments. I think that General Assembly's student-centered, practical team approach will work well with my learning style, and I think that I will be a valuable member of the team. I am looking forward to the dynamic team problem-solving that seems to be a cornerstone of your curriculum.
